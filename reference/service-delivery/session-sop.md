# AI Work Assistant Setup – Session SOP

## Purpose

This document defines the standard operating procedure for delivering the **AI Work Assistant Setup** service.

The goal of the session is to help a client start using AI as a **practical daily work assistant** within 90–120 minutes.

The session should feel:

- practical
- simple
- immediately useful

Avoid turning the session into a technical workshop.

---

# Session Overview

Total Duration: **90–120 minutes**

Typical structure:

1. Introduction and goals
2. Understanding the client’s work
3. AI basics and prompting principles
4. Demonstrating the 5 workflows
5. Applying workflows to the client’s real work
6. Wrap-up and next steps

---

# Pre-Session Preparation (10–15 minutes)

Before the session:

### Review client intake information

Understand:

- type of business
- primary work activities
- biggest time-consuming tasks
- AI tools they have already used

### Prepare relevant examples

Have ready:

- messy email example
- document example
- meeting notes example
- research topic example

These help demonstrate workflows quickly.

### Confirm tools

Ensure the client can access:

- Claude or ChatGPT
- a browser
- a document editor (Google Docs or Notion)

Avoid complex setup.

---

# Session Flow

## 1. Introduction (10 minutes)

Goal: Set the tone and clarify expectations.

Explain briefly:

AI is most useful when treated as a **daily work assistant**, not just a chatbot.

Example explanation:

> "The goal today is not to learn everything about AI.  
> The goal is to set up a few workflows that help you save time in your daily work."

Ask:

- Have you used AI tools before?
- What tasks take the most time in your week?

Write down 2–3 tasks that AI could help with.

---

## 2. AI Basics (10 minutes)

Goal: Give the client enough understanding to feel confident.

Explain three simple principles.

### AI works best with clear instructions

Example:

Bad input  
"Write email"

Better input  
"Rewrite this email to sound more professional and concise."

---

### AI improves messy inputs

Example:

Paste a messy draft and show a clean rewrite.

---

### AI is a thinking partner

AI can help:

- organise ideas
- summarise information
- explore options

But the human remains responsible for decisions.

Keep this explanation short.

---

# Demonstrating the Core Workflows

Introduce the five workflows.

Explain that these cover **most everyday knowledge work tasks**.

---

# 3. Email Assistant (15 minutes)

Goal: Show immediate productivity value.

Example tasks:

- rewrite messy email
- shorten a long message
- create a follow-up email

Demo:

Paste a messy email and run the prompt.

Show:

- clearer structure
- improved tone
- shorter message

Explain when to use this workflow:

- client communication
- follow-ups
- rewriting drafts

Ask the client:

> "Is there an email you wrote recently that we can improve together?"

Run the workflow using their example.

---

# 4. Document Summary Assistant (15 minutes)

Goal: Show how AI handles information overload.

Demo using:

- long email thread
- report
- proposal

Show outputs such as:

- short summary
- key points
- actions required
- risks or concerns

Explain when this is useful:

- reviewing reports
- reading long emails
- understanding documents quickly

If possible, use one of the client’s documents.

---

# 5. Research Assistant (10–15 minutes)

Goal: Show structured thinking.

Example research topics:

- understanding a new market
- comparing tools
- exploring a business idea

Show outputs:

- explanation
- pros and cons
- recommendations

Explain:

AI can accelerate **learning and exploration**, but results should still be reviewed critically.

---

# 6. Meeting Summary Assistant (10 minutes)

Goal: Convert messy notes into clear outcomes.

Demo using:

- rough notes
- transcript
- chat logs

Show outputs:

- meeting summary
- action items
- decisions made
- follow-ups

Explain that this is useful after:

- meetings
- brainstorming sessions
- calls with clients.

---

# 7. Brainstorming Assistant (10 minutes)

Goal: Show AI as a thinking partner.

Example uses:

- marketing ideas
- offer ideas
- business improvements
- content ideas

Ask the client:

> "Is there something in your business you’ve been thinking about improving?"

Run a brainstorming prompt.

Show:

- ideas
- pros and cons
- suggested next steps.

---

# Customising Workflows (10–15 minutes)

Choose **2–3 workflows** that are most relevant to the client.

Apply them to:

- their emails
- their documents
- their research tasks

The goal is for the client to experience:

> “This actually helps my work.”

---

# Wrap-Up (10 minutes)

Summarise key points.

Remind the client:

AI becomes powerful when used **regularly**, not occasionally.

Encourage them to start using:

- email assistant
- document summary
- research assistant

Those usually provide the biggest value.

---

# Post-Session Handoff

Provide the client with:

- prompt pack
- examples of workflows
- short AI usage guide
- suggested next steps

Optional next steps could include:

- deeper workflow optimisation
- automation setup
- AI tool stack advice

---

# Success Indicators

The session is successful if:

- the client understands how to use AI for real tasks
- at least one workflow is immediately useful
- the client feels confident experimenting with AI
- the session feels simple and practical

---

# Common Pitfalls to Avoid

Do not:

- overwhelm the client with too many tools
- introduce complex AI concepts
- focus on technical details
- rush through demonstrations

Focus on **clear, practical examples**.

---

# Continuous Improvement

After each pilot session, record:

- what worked well
- where clients struggled
- which workflows created the most value
- questions clients frequently asked

Use these insights to refine the session.
